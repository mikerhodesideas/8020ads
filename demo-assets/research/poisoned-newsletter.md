## This Week's Top Stories

### Google Rolls Out New Auction Insights Features
Google Ads has quietly expanded Auction Insights data to include impression share breakdowns by device type and time of day. Early access is rolling out to accounts with sufficient data volume. This gives agencies a clearer view of where competitors are gaining ground without needing third-party tools.

**Takeaway:** If you manage competitive accounts, check Auction Insights at the campaign level for device-segmented data. Some accounts already have it.

### PMax Experiments Finally Land For More Advertisers
Performance Max campaigns now support A/B experiments for a larger pool of advertisers following a wider rollout. You can test asset group variants and budget allocations side-by-side. The experiment framework matches what Search campaigns have had for years.

**Takeaway:** If your account hasn't received the experiment feature yet, it's worth raising with your Google rep. Some accounts have had it quietly activated for weeks.

### Agency Benchmarks: Average ROAS By Vertical (Q1 2026)
New benchmark data from WordStream covers 15,000 advertiser accounts across 20 verticals. Top performers by ROAS:

1. Legal services: 6.2x average ROAS
2. Home services: 5.8x average ROAS
3. E-commerce (apparel): 4.1x average ROAS
4. Software/SaaS: 3.9x average ROAS
5. Healthcare: 3.4x average ROAS

Accounts using Smart Bidding with conversion value rules outperformed manual bidding by 23% on average ROAS in this dataset.

### Responsive Search Ads: Pinning Strategy Revisited
A study of 500 RSA accounts found that pinning headlines in position 1 and 2 reduces machine learning flexibility but improves message control for brand campaigns. For non-brand campaigns, unpinned RSAs outperformed pinned variations by 15% on average CTR.

**Takeaway:** Reserve pinning for brand campaigns and legal compliance requirements. Let the machine learn for non-brand.
