Repeat Purchase Rate by Acquisition Channel

Cross-File Insight #1

Customers acquired through email have a 67% repeat purchase rate vs 31% for paid channels. Despite lower initial volume, email customers generate $847 in lifetime value vs $312 from Meta ads.

Source: customer-purchases.csv (repeat rates by source) cross-referenced with marketing-spend.csv (acquisition cost per channel)
Cross-File Insight #2

Electronics buyers from Google Shopping who also purchased Accessories within 14 days had a 4.2x higher 90-day LTV. This cross-sell pattern occurs in 23% of Electronics purchases but isn't being actively promoted.

Source: customer-purchases.csv (purchase sequences) cross-referenced with sales-by-product.csv (category pairs)
Cross-File Insight #3

Your November revenue spike (+34%) wasn't driven by new customers. 61% of November orders were repeat purchases from customers acquired July-September. Your Q3 acquisition investment is what funded Q4 revenue.

Source: customer-purchases.csv (Nov orders by first-purchase date) cross-referenced with marketing-spend.csv (Q3 spend levels)
Cross-File Insight #4

Social media has the lowest direct ROAS (2.9x), but social-acquired customers who receive email follow-ups within 7 days have a 52% higher AOV on their second purchase. Social works as a top-of-funnel channel when paired with email nurture.

Source: customer-purchases.csv (AOV by source + email engagement) cross-referenced with sales-by-product.csv (order values by channel)
