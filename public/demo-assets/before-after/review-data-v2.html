Deep Dive

Diminishing Returns
Google Ads: Spend vs Marginal CPA
Finding: Google Ads shows clear diminishing returns above $14K/month. CPA increases 34% when monthly spend exceeds this threshold, while lead quality (measured by close rate) drops from 21% to 14%. Recommend capping Google Ads at $13K/month and reallocating the surplus to Email and Events.

Cross-File Intelligence

Correlations Across Datasets
Cross-File Insight #1
Email's 8.2x ROAS is being throttled by under-investment. At just 8.6% of total spend, it generates 18.3% of all leads and 24.1% of closed revenue.
Evidence: marketing-spend.csv shows $42K allocated to email. lead-pipeline.csv shows 521 leads attributed. closed-deals.csv shows $344K in email-sourced revenue, with a 26.1% close rate (vs 18.3% portfolio average).
Cross-File Insight #2
Events leads close at 2.3x the rate of paid channels, and their average deal size is $5,840 vs $3,420 for Google Ads.
Evidence: closed-deals.csv shows 71 event-sourced deals with avg value $5,840. Google Ads shows 170 deals at avg $3,420. Event leads also move through pipeline 18 days faster per lead-pipeline.csv stage timestamps.
Cross-File Insight #3
Q3 spend spike ($48K in August) delivered the highest revenue month ($218K) but with a 6-week lag, not the expected 2-week window.
Evidence: marketing-spend.csv August total $48K (13% above monthly average). Revenue peaked in October at $234K per closed-deals.csv, not August. lead-pipeline.csv confirms the pipeline build time averaged 42 days for Q3 leads.
Cross-File Insight #4
Cutting email spend by 15% in Q4 (from $4.2K to $3.1K/mo) preceded a $26K revenue drop in the following quarter. The compound effect cost 6.3x the savings.
Evidence: marketing-spend.csv shows email budget reduced Oct-Dec. closed-deals.csv shows email-sourced revenue dropped from $98K (Q3) to $72K (Q4). lead-pipeline.csv shows new email leads fell 31% in Q4, compounding into Q1 pipeline thinning.
