Cross-File Insight #1
Email campaigns show a 60-day revenue lag
By joining marketing-spend.csv timestamps with closed-deals.csv close dates, email-sourced leads consistently convert 58-63 days after initial spend. This means January email investment drives March revenue. Current attribution models undervalue email by an estimated 34%.
Cross-File Insight #2
Event-sourced leads have 2.3x higher lifetime value
Matching lead-pipeline.csv source tags with closed-deals.csv revenue data reveals event leads close at $6,412 average deal size vs $2,788 for other channels. They also show 41% higher repeat purchase rate within 12 months.
Cross-File Insight #3
September delivered best ROAS after August conference
The August industry conference ($18K sponsorship) created a pipeline surge visible in September's 5.3x blended ROAS, the highest monthly figure. Conference leads entered lead-pipeline.csv in August but closed in September, showing in closed-deals.csv with a 22-day average sales cycle vs 38-day norm.
Cross-File Insight #4
Google Ads shows diminishing returns above $14K/month
Plotting monthly Google Ads spend from marketing-spend.csv against corresponding lead volume in lead-pipeline.csv, incremental cost-per-lead rises 47% once monthly spend exceeds $14K. The marginal ROAS above that threshold drops to 1.9x vs 4.6x below it.

Invest in Q3 conference sponsorship

The August conference generated a measurable September ROAS spike. Securing a larger sponsorship package ($24K vs $18K) with a dedicated lead capture workflow could amplify the effect. Event leads' 2.3x LTV multiplier makes higher upfront cost justifiable.

LOW

Test content syndication partnerships

Content's 3.2x ROAS is the lowest performer, but lead quality data from closed-deals.csv shows content leads have comparable close rates when nurtured. Testing 2-3 syndication partners over Q2 could improve volume without proportional cost increase. Start with a $2K/month pilot.

Expected impact: +40-60 leads/month at current CPL, data for Q3 decision
