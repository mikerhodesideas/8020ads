PHASE 1 Day 1: Welcome & Access

Send personalised welcome email
Owner: Account Manager Time: 10 min Tool: Gmail
  • Confirm engagement scope and monthly fee
  • Attach signed agreement as PDF
  • Include your direct phone number and response SLA (within 4 hours)
  • Link to the shared reporting dashboard (set up in Task 4)
TEMPLATE: welcome-email-v3.md
Request Google Ads admin access
Owner: Account Manager Time: 5 min Tool: Google Ads UI
  • Send invitation from your MCC account
  • Request Admin level (not Standard; you'll need billing visibility)
  • Confirm client has accepted invite before proceeding to audit
Request Google Analytics & GTM access
Owner: Account Manager Time: 5 min Tool: GA4 / GTM
  • GA4: request Editor role on the property
  • GTM: request Publish access to the container
  • If client doesn't have GTM, flag for setup in Phase 2
Set up shared reporting dashboard
Owner: Analyst Time: 30 min Tool: Looker Studio
  • Clone the client dashboard template
  • Connect Google Ads and GA4 data sources
  • Set date range to last 90 days for baseline
  • Share with client (viewer access)
TEMPLATE: looker-studio-client-v2

PHASE 2 Day 2-3: Discovery & Audit

Run full account audit
Owner: Strategist Time: 2 hrs Tool: Google Ads + audit script
  • Campaign structure assessment (naming, segmentation)
  • Search term relevance check (last 90 days)
  • Negative keyword coverage review
  • Conversion tracking validation (are conversions real?)
  • Budget vs. impression share analysis
Kickoff call with client
Owner: Account Manager + Strategist Time: 45 min Tool: Zoom
  • Walk through audit findings (screen share the dashboard)
  • Confirm business goals, target CPA, and ROAS expectations
  • Identify their top 3 services/products by margin
  • Agree on first 30-day action plan
  • Set recurring meeting cadence (biweekly recommended)
TEMPLATE: kickoff-agenda-v2.md
Document client context in your system
Owner: Account Manager Time: 20 min Tool: CRM / Brain
  • Business type, service area, seasonality notes
  • Key contacts (who approves ad copy, who approves budget?)
  • Competitor names they care about
  • Past agency history and what went wrong

PHASE 3 Week 1: Foundation Setup

Implement conversion tracking fixes
Owner: Analyst Time: 1-3 hrs Tool: GTM / GA4
  • Verify all conversion actions fire correctly (test with Tag Assistant)
  • Remove duplicate conversion actions
  • Set primary vs. secondary conversion classification
  • Set up enhanced conversions if applicable
Build negative keyword lists
Owner: Strategist Time: 1 hr Tool: Google Ads + neg keyword script
  • Create shared negative keyword lists (brand terms, competitor terms, irrelevant)
  • Apply across all Search campaigns
  • Schedule weekly search term review (every Monday)
Restructure campaigns (if needed)
Owner: Strategist Time: 2-4 hrs Tool: Google Ads
  • Consolidate thin ad groups (under 10 clicks/week)
  • Split overloaded campaigns by intent or service
  • Standardise naming convention

PHASE 4 Week 2: Optimise & Confirm

Write and launch new ad copy
Owner: Strategist Time: 2 hrs Tool: Google Ads
  • 2 new RSAs per ad group (minimum)
  • Include client's unique value props from kickoff call
  • Pin top-performing headlines to Position 1
  • Get client approval on ad copy before launch
Set bid strategy and targets
Owner: Strategist Time: 30 min Tool: Google Ads
  • Align bid strategy with agreed goals (tCPA, tROAS, or Max Conversions)
  • Set targets based on audit baseline, not guesswork
  • Document targets in the client profile
Send Week 2 progress report
Owner: Account Manager Time: 20 min Tool: Email + Dashboard
  • Summary of changes made and why
  • Early performance indicators vs. baseline
  • Next 2 weeks' plan
  • Remind them of next scheduled call

PITFALLS Common Mistakes

Not verifying conversion tracking before optimising
If conversions are double-counting or missing, every optimisation decision is based on bad data. Always validate tracking in Phase 2, before making changes.
Making big changes without baseline data
Export 90-day performance before touching anything. You need a "before" to prove your "after" is better. Clients will ask.
Skipping the kickoff call context questions
If you don't ask about margins, seasonality, and past agency problems, you'll repeat the same mistakes the last agency made.
Promising specific results in Week 1
Set expectations that Week 1-2 is foundation work. Performance gains typically show in Week 3-4. Overpromising early leads to churn.
Not documenting the agreed plan
If the 30-day plan isn't written down and shared, the client will remember a different version. Always send a follow-up email after kickoff.

SUCCESS End of Week 2 Checkpoints

Client can access their live dashboard
They've opened it at least once and know where to find it. No "where's my report?" emails.
Conversion tracking is validated and clean
No duplicates, no missing events. Primary conversions correctly classified. You trust the data.
Client knows the 30-day plan and next call date
They received the kickoff summary email. They replied or acknowledged. They know what you're doing and why.
At least one optimisation is live
New ad copy, negative keywords, or restructured campaigns. Something tangible is running. Momentum matters.
Client context is documented in your system
If you got hit by a bus, another team member could pick up this account tomorrow and know the goals, contacts, and history.
