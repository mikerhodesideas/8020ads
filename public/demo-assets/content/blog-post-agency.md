# Why We Stopped Selling Hourly PPC Management

Two years ago, I sat across from a client who'd been with us for 18 months and heard the words every agency owner dreads: "I just don't see what we're paying for."

His account was performing well. ROAS was 4.2x, up from 2.8x when we took over. We'd restructured his campaigns, built a proper negative keyword strategy, launched Performance Max with clean asset groups, and cut his cost per lead by 31%.

But his invoice said "42 hours at £95/hour." And his question was reasonable: "What did you actually do for 42 hours?"

That conversation changed how we run the agency.

## The Problem With Hours

We're a team of 12 managing around 30 accounts. For years, our model was straightforward: charge an hourly rate, track time meticulously, send detailed timesheets with every invoice. We thought transparency would build trust. It did the opposite.

Clients didn't care about our hours. They cared about their results. And when you bill by the hour, every conversation becomes about time, not outcomes. "Why did that take six hours?" "Can we reduce hours next month?" "Do you really need to spend three hours on reporting?"

The worst part was what it did to our team. Senior strategists who could restructure an account in four hours felt guilty charging less than a junior who took twelve hours to do the same work. Speed and expertise were punished. Inefficiency was rewarded.

We also noticed a pattern: our most profitable clients were the ones where we spent the least time. The accounts were well-built, the strategies were solid, and they mostly needed monitoring and incremental optimisation. But under an hourly model, those clients felt like they were getting less value because their invoices were smaller.

## The Switch

In January 2024, we moved to value-based pricing. Not overnight, and not without losing some clients. But here's what we did.

We designed three tiers:

**Essentials (£1,800/month)**
- Up to £15,000 monthly ad spend
- Weekly optimisation
- Monthly performance report
- Quarterly strategy review
- Covers one advertising platform

**Growth (£3,500/month)**
- Up to £50,000 monthly ad spend
- Twice-weekly optimisation
- Fortnightly performance calls
- Monthly strategy sessions
- Covers up to two platforms
- Landing page recommendations
- Competitor monitoring

**Scale (£6,000/month)**
- Ad spend above £50,000
- Daily monitoring
- Weekly performance calls
- Bi-monthly strategy days (half-day sessions)
- Cross-platform (Google, Meta, LinkedIn)
- CRO recommendations
- Creative strategy input
- Dedicated account strategist

## What Happened Next

Three clients left immediately. All three were on the lower end of our spend range and had been paying roughly £1,200/month on hourly billing. The jump to £1,800 was too much for them, or more accurately, they valued hours more than outcomes. Two of them came back within four months after their new agencies failed to maintain performance.

The remaining 27 clients moved to tiers within two billing cycles. Most landed on Growth or Scale. Nobody argued about hours anymore. Conversations shifted entirely to performance, strategy, and growth opportunities.

Here's what the numbers looked like after 12 months:

| Metric | Hourly Model | Tiered Model | Change |
|--------|-------------|--------------|--------|
| Average revenue per client | £2,840/mo | £3,720/mo | +31% |
| Monthly recurring revenue | £85,200 | £111,600 | +31% |
| Client retention (12-month) | 72% | 91% | +19pts |
| Team utilisation rate | 87% | 74% | -13pts |
| Profit margin | 18% | 34% | +16pts |

The utilisation rate dropping was the most counterintuitive result, and the most important. Our team was working fewer hours per client but delivering better outcomes because they weren't padding timesheets or second-guessing whether to spend an extra hour on something that needed doing.

## The Conversations Changed

This was the part I didn't expect. Under the hourly model, client calls were defensive. We'd spend the first ten minutes justifying time entries and the last ten minutes rushing through actual strategy.

Under the tiered model, every call became about performance and opportunity. "Should we test this new audience?" "Here's what your competitors are doing." "Let's look at reallocating budget from Search to PMax based on last month's data."

One client told me, and I'm paraphrasing: "I used to dread our monthly calls because I knew I'd feel like I was being billed for the privilege of being sold to. Now I actually look forward to them."

That single comment justified the entire transition.

## Handling the Objections

Not everyone was convinced. The three most common pushbacks:

**"But what if you just do less work?"**
Fair concern. We built in specific deliverables for each tier (weekly reports, strategy sessions, etc.) and added a performance guarantee: if their primary KPI declined for two consecutive months and it was within our control, we'd provide a month of service at no charge. We've invoked it once in two years.

**"This is more expensive than what I was paying."**
For some clients, yes. But we reframed it: "You were paying £2,400/month for hours. You're now paying £3,500/month for a 4.2x ROAS on £40,000 spend. The fee is 8.75% of ad spend, and we're returning £168,000 in revenue. Is £3,500 the right question, or is £168,000?"

Most clients got it immediately.

**"What about scope creep? What if I need more?"**
Each tier has clear boundaries. If a Growth client needs daily monitoring or CRO work, they move to Scale. Simple. No awkward conversations about "that'll be extra hours." Just a clear upgrade path.

## What I'd Tell Other Agency Owners

If you're still billing by the hour, you're competing on cost. And there will always be someone cheaper. If you're competing on cost, you're not competing on value.

The transition isn't easy. You'll lose clients who valued cheap hours over good outcomes. You'll have uncomfortable conversations. Your team will resist at first because timesheets feel safe, even when they're miserable to fill out.

But here's what's on the other side: predictable revenue, better client relationships, higher margins, and a team that's focused on doing great work instead of counting minutes.

We went from £85,200 to £111,600 in monthly recurring revenue, improved our profit margin from 18% to 34%, and our client retention went from 72% to 91%. And nobody, not once in the last year, has asked me what we did for 42 hours.

That question simply doesn't exist anymore.
