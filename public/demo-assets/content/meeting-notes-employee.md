## Q1 Performance + Budget Reallocation Discussion

**10:04am** - Q1 overall numbers
Shared the deck I prepped:
- total paid spend Q1: $142K across Google, LinkedIn, Meta
- total MQLs: 847
- blended CPL: $167
- pipeline influenced: $1.8M (attribution is fuzzy but directionally right)
- overall ROAS across all channels: 3.2x

broke it down by channel:

**Google Ads ($68K spend, 48% of budget):**
- Brand: $12K spend, 312 MQLs, $38 CPL, **8.1x ROAS** - the star
- Generic: $34K spend, 298 MQLs, $114 CPL, 2.8x ROAS - solid
- Competitor: $22K spend, 89 MQLs, $247 CPL, 1.4x ROAS - eh

**LinkedIn ($42K spend, 30% of budget):**
- Lead gen forms: $28K spend, 152 MQLs, $184 CPL
- Sponsored content: $14K spend, 75 MQLs, $187 CPL
- combined ROAS: 0.9x
- yes, sub-1x. I know.

**Meta ($32K spend, 22% of budget):**
- Retargeting: $18K spend, 124 MQLs, $145 CPL, 3.6x ROAS
- Prospecting: $14K spend, 97 MQLs, $144 CPL, 1.2x ROAS

Sarah seemed fine with the numbers overall but zeroed in on LinkedIn immediately. as expected.

**10:18am** - the LinkedIn problem
Sarah: "we're spending $42K a quarter on LinkedIn and the ROAS is below 1?"
- I had the data ready for this. LinkedIn CPL is $185 avg vs $42 on Google Brand
- LinkedIn audience quality argument: "they're more senior, longer sales cycle"
- but honestly the data doesn't support that anymore
- pulled up the SQL pipeline report - LinkedIn MQLs convert to SQL at 8% vs Google at 14%
- even accounting for deal size, LinkedIn isn't paying for itself

James jumped in hard here:
- James: "the LinkedIn leads download the whitepaper and ghost us. the SDRs are burning cycles on people who were never going to buy"
- said his team tracks it - of 227 LinkedIn MQLs in Q1, SDRs got meetings with 31. closed 2.
- James: "that's a 0.9% close rate. I could get better numbers cold calling."
- Sarah didn't love hearing that but she asked what the Google numbers look like
- Google MQLs: met with 89 out of 687, closed 19. close rate 2.8%.
- the room got quiet for a sec

**10:32am** - reallocation proposal
Presented my recommendation:
- cut LinkedIn by 50% ($42K -> $21K per quarter)
- move $15K/month to Google Generic (scale what's working)
- move $6K/month to Meta Retargeting (best ROAS outside of Brand)
- net effect: same total budget, better expected ROAS

Sarah's concern: "what happens to our brand presence on LinkedIn if we cut spend?" told her organic is free, maintain presence without paid. she's worried about the board seeing us "pull back on LinkedIn" since they pushed for it last year. suggested framing it as "optimizing channel mix" not "cutting LinkedIn"
- James: "I'll back you on this in the board meeting if it means my team gets better leads"

**DECISION:** test the reallocation for Q2
- LinkedIn cut to $21K/quarter (keep lead gen forms, pause sponsored content)
- Google Generic gets an extra $15K/quarter
- Meta Retargeting gets an extra $6K/quarter
- revisit in 8 weeks with Q2 data

Sarah wants weekly reports during the transition period. fair enough.
