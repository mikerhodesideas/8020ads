# How We Cut Our Google Ads Waste by 34% (Without Touching Our Bids)

Last quarter, one of our clients was spending $42,000 a month on Google Ads. Their campaigns were profitable, their ROAS looked healthy, and nobody was complaining.

But when we looked under the hood, we found $14,280 per month going to clicks that would never convert. Not "maybe won't convert." Never. Irrelevant search terms, duplicate audiences, campaigns competing against each other, and budget draining into time slots where their phone wasn't even staffed.

Here's exactly how we found it and fixed it, step by step.

## The Client: Thunderbolt Electricals

Thunderbolt Electricals is a commercial and residential electrical contractor based in Melbourne. They run Search, Performance Max, and Display campaigns across Google Ads, spending roughly $42K/month. Their target cost per lead was $18. They were hitting $21.40 when we started the audit.

By the end of this process, their CPL dropped to $14.10 and their monthly waste fell from $14,280 to under $2,000.

## Step 1: Audit Search Terms Ruthlessly

The single biggest source of waste in almost every Google Ads account is search terms. Not keywords. Search terms.

We pulled 90 days of search term data and found that 23% of their spend was going to terms that had zero conversions across the entire period. Not low conversion rate. Zero.

Examples of what we found:

- "electrician salary australia" (informational, zero purchase intent)
- "how to wire a light switch" (DIY searchers, not customers)
- "cheap electrician" (attracted price shoppers who never booked)
- Competitor brand names triggering broad match

We added 847 negative keywords in the first week. That alone cut spend by $4,600/month with no drop in conversions.

**The rule we follow:** If a search term has spent more than 2x your target CPA with zero conversions, it's a negative keyword. No debate. No "let's give it more time." Add it and move on.

## Step 2: Kill Zombie Campaigns

Thunderbolt had 14 active campaigns. Four of them had produced exactly 3 conversions between them in the last 90 days, while consuming $3,200/month in budget.

These zombie campaigns had been "testing" for months. Nobody had reviewed them. They were just quietly burning cash.

We paused all four. Conversions didn't change. Cost dropped immediately.

**The check:** Sort all campaigns by conversions over the last 90 days. Anything with fewer than 5 conversions and more than $500 spend needs to justify its existence. If it can't, pause it.

## Step 3: Fix the Ad Schedule

This one surprised even us. Thunderbolt's campaigns were running 24/7, but their office closed at 5pm and didn't open until 7:30am. They had no after-hours call answering service.

We pulled hourly performance data and found that 18% of clicks were happening between 9pm and 6am. The conversion rate during those hours was 0.4%, compared to 3.8% during business hours.

We didn't turn off ads completely overnight (some emergency electrical searches are genuine), but we reduced bids by 80% from 9pm to 6am and redirected that budget to the 7am-11am window where their conversion rate peaked at 5.2%.

Savings: roughly $2,100/month.

## Step 4: Eliminate Audience Overlap

Their Performance Max campaigns and Search campaigns were competing for the same audiences. PMax was bidding on branded terms that Search was already covering, and two separate PMax campaigns had near-identical audience signals.

We restructured the PMax campaigns with proper brand exclusions and consolidated overlapping audience signals into a single campaign with distinct asset groups.

This reduced their cost per lead on PMax from $24.60 to $16.30 and eliminated the internal auction competition that was inflating their CPCs by an estimated 15%.

## Step 5: Landing Page Alignment

This isn't technically an "ads" fix, but it accounted for a measurable chunk of waste. Three of their top-spending ad groups were sending traffic to the homepage instead of service-specific landing pages.

The homepage had a 2.1% conversion rate. The service pages converted at 4.7%. Same traffic source, same keywords, more than double the conversion rate just by matching the landing page to the search intent.

We estimate this single change saved $3,800/month in wasted clicks that would have bounced off the homepage.

The total spend actually decreased, but leads went up because budget was flowing to search terms and time slots that actually convert.

## The Takeaway

Most Google Ads accounts don't have a bidding problem. They have a waste problem. The money is there, it's just going to the wrong places.

Before you start testing new bid strategies, adding more keywords, or increasing budgets, do the boring work first:

1. Pull 90 days of search term data and add negatives aggressively
2. Kill campaigns that haven't converted in 90 days
3. Check your ad schedule against your actual business hours
4. Look for audience overlap between campaigns
5. Make sure every ad group points to a relevant landing page

These five steps won't take more than a few hours. And in our experience across dozens of accounts, they typically recover 15-35% of wasted spend.

The best part: none of this requires changing your bid strategy, switching campaign types, or rebuilding anything from scratch. It's just cleaning up what's already there.
